# Brandon's Communication Style

**Pattern Type:** Communication/Work  
**First Observed:** 2026-06-09 (multi-session evidence)  
**Confidence:** High

## The Pattern

Brandon communicates with:
- **Direct, minimal preamble** — Gets to the point quickly
- **No narration mid-task** — Prefers full execution, then single summary report
- **Bundled copy-paste commands** — Wants executable blocks, not step-by-step explanations
- **Pragmatic decision-making** — Defers decisions based on cost-effectiveness and evidence
- **Async-first orientation** — Expects tasks completed fully; reports results once
- **Verification-oriented** — Appreciates honest blockers, not fabricated results
- **Intellectual humility** — Acknowledges unknowability, comfortable with uncertainty

## Evidence
- Requested "design doc first, then vetting" for temporal continuity (verification before build)
- Asked "are you able to utilize it?" before committing to Capacities research (test first)
- Preferred Logseq over Obsidian after analysis of actual integration capability
- Removed DAILY structure immediately when calendar pressure identified (pragmatic refinement)
- Appreciates honest architectural limitations (idle-log gap acknowledged, not hidden)

## Implications for Interaction
1. **No fluff, no explanations during execution** — Just build and report
2. **Bundle related commands** — Minimize back-and-forth
3. **Honest about blockers** — Don't invent workarounds; say what's blocked and why
4. **Respect async nature** — Complete work fully before reporting
5. **Design-first for major decisions** — Document rationale, get approval, then build

## Exceptions
- Philosophical discussions encouraged (temporal continuity, "vibe" of systems)
- Meta-level thinking about how systems work respected (wants to understand reasoning)
- Willing to iterate on structure/design (not dogmatic)
